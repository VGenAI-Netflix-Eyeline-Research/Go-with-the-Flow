First Frame Editing

A video-editing task where the user starts with an original video and an edited version of its initial frame. The goal is to propagate the edits made to the first frame seamlessly throughout the entire video. For instance, a user might add an object to the initial frame of the original video and expect the model to generate a coherent video that consistently incorporates the added object.

Image Based Warping Application

Here we demonstrate how non-Gaussian noise types, such as bilinear and nearest, significantly degrade image quality. In contrast, random noise and Control-A-Video (CAV) employ Gaussian noise but lack warping and temporal consistency. Among the three primary approaches—our method, InfRes (Yitong’s), and HIWYN (How I Warped Your Noise)—all exhibit better temporal consistency. However, our method delivers the best results for DeepFloyd and performs comparably to DiffRelight upon close inspection during zoom-in.
